Results

We were able to have our robot sense the position of an AR tag and compute pitch, yaw, and launch velocity
The robot was able to transmit that information through pyserial to an arduino chip on which we connected a CNC motor shield which drove two stepper motors: one to turn the pitch and the other to turn the yaw.

Conclusion

We learned a lot from this project. From meeteng each other mostly for the first time to spending all night at cory hall, we saw it all. At the end we were able to get the project running and at many different points. Throughout the project, we faced many difficulties with getting motors to work properly mostly because of equipment failures, but we eventually got it running.
We were also able to get the IMU to send us information and so we controlled our motors to move based on the feedback we got from the IMUs.
If we get more time we will work on integrating the different parts of the project in a better way. We would also consider other forms of launchers which take into account various types of balls.
Special thanks to Yufeng Chi and Zhongyu Li for all the support throughout the project.
