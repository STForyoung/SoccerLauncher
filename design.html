Sensing

We used openCV to get the pose of our target from AR tag. We then calculated the transformation between our camera frame and the target frame. To get our robot's pitch and yaw angles we use an IMU and an Arduino.


Planning

We first calculate the desired pitch and yaw angles using kinematic formulas. To rotate pitch and yaw such that we are aiming correctly, we continuously rotate our stepper motors in the desired direction and check whether we have exceeded our goal every 200 cycles. We also implemented an alternate approach that involves calculating the required steps directly using information about the pitch of the lead screws and chain and sprocket.


Actuation

To change pitch and yaw we used two NEMA 23 Stepper motors. One rotates a sprocket and chain that connect to center of the launcher. another motor rotates the lead screws that raise and lower the pitch of the launching rails. To shoot the ball we used two DC Brush Motors.
